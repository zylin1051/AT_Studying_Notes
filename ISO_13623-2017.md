# ISO 13623 - The Design of Pipeline System for the Transportation of Petroleum/natural Gas

## Original Document

View the document (2017 version) [here](ISO_13623-2017.pdf).

## Scope

- Parent standards for pipeline design, construction, testing, opeation, maintenance, and abandonment
- Petroleum and natural gas (fluids) transportation
- Rigid, metallic pipelines (not application to flexible pipeline structures)
- On-land and offshore
- See **Figure 2 on Page 2** for the extent of pipeline systems covered


## Relevant External/Child References
- [ISO 15589-1](ISO_15589-1-2015.md) - Cathodic Protection of *On-land* Pipelines
- [ISO 15589-2](ISO_15589-2-2024.md) - Cathodic Protection of *Offshore* Pipelines
- [ISO 21809-3](ISO_21809-3-2016.md) - Field Joint Coatings


## Things to Consider when Referencing ISO 13623

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Is this for petroleum/natural gas pipeline transportation? | If yes, ISO 13623 may be relevant. |
| Is it on-land or offshore? | Both can be covered. |
| Is it a rigid metallic pipeline? | If yes, it fits the material/type scope. |
| Is it a new system or a modification? | New systems are directly covered; modifications can be covered. |
| Is the item part of the pipeline system or only plant/process piping? | This decides whether ISO 13623 or another piping/process standard controls. |
| **Is the item pipe, valve, bend, flange, fitting, coating, CP equipment, or station equipment?** | **Different referenced standards apply (see Section 2).** |
| Is the reference dated or undated? | This determines which edition applies. |
| Does the client specification override or add to ISO 13623? | Project specs often add stricter requirements. |
| Are there local regulations? | ISO 13623 allows national requirements for safety/environment to apply. |
| Is the item within the pipeline system or inside a station/terminal? | This affects whether it is “pipeline” or “piping” under **Section 3**. |

## Working Guideline

- **CP** = Cathodic Protection
- **PJP** = Pipeline Joint Protection
- **SC** = Structural Coatings

| Area / Topic | Proposal Guideline | Why It Matters | Watch-Out / Clarification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Applicability | Confirm whether ISO 13623 applies to the project scope. | ISO 13623 applies to pipeline transportation systems, not every coating, piping, or structural item. | Do not apply ISO 13623 blindly to structural steel, plant piping, flexible pipe, or non-metallic pipe unless the project specifically requires it. |
| Scope / Battery Limits | Define exactly what is included in the company scope: CP, PJP, SC, design, supply, installation, inspection, testing, or documentation. | ISO 13623 defines the pipeline system broadly, including corrosion protection systems, stations, SCADA, safety systems, and related facilities. | Avoid accepting “pipeline system” responsibility without clearly limiting the proposed scope. |
| Compliance Wording | Use controlled wording such as “applicable requirements of ISO 13623:2017 within the stated proposal scope.” | Prevents over-claiming full pipeline-system compliance when only part of the system is supplied. | Avoid simply stating “complies with ISO 13623” unless the full responsibility and exclusions are clear. |
| Child Standards | Identify which detailed standards apply after ISO 13623 is triggered. | ISO 13623 is an umbrella standard and often points to more detailed standards. | Confirm editions. Client specifications may require a different or older/newer edition. |
| CP Scope | For CP work, use ISO 13623 mainly to identify the CP requirement, then use ISO 15589 for detailed CP design and monitoring. | ISO 13623 points CP systems to ISO 15589-1 for on-land pipelines and ISO 15589-2 for offshore pipelines. | Do not price CP based only on ISO 13623; it does not contain full CP design calculations. |
| Pipeline Joint Protection | For field joint coating, check ISO 13623 Section 10.5 and ISO 21809-3. | Field joint coating is a construction-critical corrosion protection activity. | Do not quote only coating materials unless application, inspection, holiday testing, repair, and documentation are clearly excluded. |
| Plant-Applied vs Field Joint Coating | Separate parent/plant-applied coating from field joint coating. | Plant-applied coating and field joint coating are governed by different technical requirements. | ISO 21809-3 is for field joint coatings, not plant-applied coating. |
| Structural Coatings | Confirm whether structural coating work is actually governed by ISO 13623 or by a project coating specification. | ISO 13623 is not a complete structural coating specification. | For supports, skids, tanks, platforms, or pipe racks, request the project/client structural coating standard. |
| Design Inputs | Request minimum design inputs before pricing. | CP and coating quantities depend on project-specific conditions. | Confirm design life, MAOP, design pressure, temperature, fluid, onshore/offshore, soil/seawater data, coating type, CP philosophy, and monitoring requirements. |
| Corrosion Management | Use Section 9 to identify corrosion threats, mitigation, monitoring, inspection, and documentation requirements. | Corrosion control is a lifecycle requirement, not just a product supply item. | Do not quote only hardware if the project expects corrosion management deliverables, reports, or monitoring plans. |
| External Coating Selection | Confirm coating type, design temperature, service environment, installation method, damage resistance, repair method, and CP compatibility. | Coating performance directly affects CP demand and long-term integrity. | Watch for cathodic disbondment, poor adhesion, installation damage, thermal cycling, and incompatible field joint systems. |
| CP Shielding | Review bedding, casings, sleeves, HDD protection, concrete protection, rock shield, and mechanical protection. | Mechanical protection can sometimes block CP current from reaching the pipe surface. | Clarify responsibility for evaluating CP shielding risk. |
| Construction Damage | Clarify who is responsible for coating/anode damage during transport, handling, stringing, bending, lowering-in, backfilling, offshore laying, and crossings. | ISO 13623 requires damaged coating to be inspected and repaired before final installation. | Construction damage responsibility is a common commercial gap. |
| Holiday Detection | Confirm whether holiday testing is included and at what stages. | Holidays can expose steel and increase CP demand. | Include acceptance criteria, repair method, retesting, and reporting requirements. |
| Isolating Couplings / Isolation Joints | Confirm pressure rating, electrical isolation testing, material compatibility, and installation requirements. | ISO 13623 requires fabricated isolating couplings to be pressure-tested and electrically tested before installation. | Include test certificates and electrical test requirements in the proposal scope. |
| O&M / Monitoring | Check whether CP monitoring, corrosion monitoring, leak detection, pigging, ILI support, test stations, or survey access are required. | ISO 13623 requires O&M requirements to be considered during design. | Do not leave monitoring and access requirements undefined. |
| Hydrotest / Pre-commissioning | Clarify whether hydrotest water quality, inhibitors, biocides, oxygen scavengers, drying, cleaning, gauging, and preservation are in scope. | Test water and retained water can create internal corrosion risk or affect coatings/linings. | Confirm compatibility with internal coatings, seals, valves, and downstream fluid requirements. |
| Documentation | Define required proposal deliverables and project records. | ISO 13623 emphasizes lifetime records and traceability. | Include ITPs, APS, PQT/PPT records, coating reports, holiday test reports, repair logs, CP design reports, CP survey records, material certificates, and as-built data where applicable. |
| Deviations | List all deviations, assumptions, exclusions, and client clarifications. | Prevents later disputes over scope and compliance. | Any missing data should be stated as an assumption or clarification item. | |

## Minimum Bid Clarification Questions

- **CP** = Cathodic Protection
- **PJP** = Pipeline Joint Protection
- **SC** = Structural Coatings

| Area | Clarification Question |
|---|---|
| Scope | Is the requested scope CP, field joint coating, plant-applied coating, coating repair, structural coating, monitoring, installation, or design only? |
| Pipeline Type | Is the pipeline on-land or offshore? Buried, submerged, above-ground, cased, HDD, J-tube, riser, or station piping? |
| Operating Data | What are the design life, MAOP, design pressure, operating temperature range, and shut-in conditions? |
| Fluid | What is the transported fluid category? Is there H₂S, CO₂, water, oxygen, MIC risk, sour service, or multiphase flow? |
| Coating | What is the parent coating system, field joint coating system, design temperature, cutback, overlap, and compatibility requirement? |
| CP | Is CP on-land or offshore? Galvanic or impressed current? What are the design criteria, monitoring requirements, isolation philosophy, and current drains? |
| Construction | Who is responsible for handling damage, holiday detection, coating repair, anode installation, and final inspection? |
| Testing | Are hydrotest water quality, inhibitors, drying, pigging/gauging, and post-test preservation in scope? |
| Documentation | What deliverables are required: design report, ITP, APS, PQT/PPT, inspection reports, as-built drawings, CP survey records, coating repair logs, certificates? |
| Deviations | Are there any client/project requirements stricter than ISO 13623 or different from the ISO child standards? | |
