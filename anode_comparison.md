# Common CP Anode Categories

The table below compares common anode categories by their basic meaning, typical CP type, product form, applications, advantages, and key points to remember.

| Anode Category | Simple Meaning | Usually CP Type | Typical Product Form | Typical Applications | Main Advantage | Key Point to Remember |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Probe Anode** | A compact anode inserted into a specific location to provide localized protection. | Usually impressed current | Rod / probe / cartridge type | Tanks, vessels, internal surfaces, confined areas, localized CP zones | Compact and easy to install in limited-access areas | Best for **localized protection**, not usually for long continuous structures. |
| **Sacrificial Anode** | An anode that naturally consumes itself to protect the steel structure. | Galvanic / sacrificial CP | Block, bracelet, rod, plate, embedded type, etc. | Pipelines, tanks, marine structures, offshore structures, reinforced concrete repair | No external power supply required | “Sacrificial” describes **how it works**, not its shape. |
| **Ribbon Anode** | A long, narrow strip-type anode used to distribute current along a structure. | Can be sacrificial or impressed current, depending on design | Continuous ribbon / strip | Pipelines, AC mitigation, grounding, distributed CP, narrow spaces | Provides more uniform current distribution along a length | “Ribbon” describes the **shape**, not necessarily the CP principle. |
| **Flexible Anode** | A cable-like anode that can be installed along irregular or long structures. | Usually impressed current | Flexible cable / continuous linear anode | Long buried pipelines, tank bottoms, complex layouts, high-resistivity environments | Very good current distribution and installation flexibility | Usually needs a power source and proper current control. |
| **Mesh Anode** | A mesh-shaped anode that spreads current over a surface area. | Usually impressed current | Expanded mesh / grid | Reinforced concrete, tank bottoms, surface-area CP systems | Good current distribution over broad areas | Best when protection needs to be spread across a surface, not just along a line. |
| **Embedded Anode** | An anode installed inside repair mortar or concrete near the steel reinforcement. | Usually sacrificial | Small embedded unit | Concrete repair, bridge decks, columns, marine concrete structures | Helps control rebar corrosion after concrete repair | Mainly used for **reinforced concrete**, not general pipeline CP. |
| **Rigid Impressed Current Anode** | A solid, non-flexible anode connected to an external power source. | Impressed current | Rod, tubular, or cast body | Deep-well groundbeds, soil CP systems, industrial CP systems | Durable and suitable for fixed groundbed installations | Strong but less flexible; installation layout is less adjustable. |

## How to Understand Anode Categories

| Classification Question | Example Categories |
|---|---|
| **How does it work?** | Sacrificial anode, impressed current anode |
| **What shape/design is it?** | Probe, ribbon, mesh, flexible, rod, tubular |
| **Where is it installed?** | Embedded, internal, external, groundbed, concrete, pipeline |
